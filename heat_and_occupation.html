    • Percentage changeWhen you have data for two points in time, you can calculate how much change there has been during this period. The result is expressed as a percentage (in absolute numbers, it’s just a difference) and is called the rate of change, i.e. the percentage change. It is calculated as follows: [(Number at later time ÷ Number at earlier time) - 1] × 100.

      Example In 2010, there were 4.8 million persons employed in country X and in 2015 there were 5.2 million. The percentage change from 2010 to 2015 is then calculated as follows: [(5.2 ÷ 4.8)-1] x 100= +8.3%
